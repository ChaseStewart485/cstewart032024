Believe the Hype About Quantum Security: Report

John P. Mello Jr.

A new report from Forrester Research emphasizes the importance of organizations preparing for quantum security threats, even though quantum computers that can break current encryption may not be available for 5 to 10 years. The report warns that hackers are already using a “harvest now, decrypt later” approach, where they store encrypted data today and wait for quantum computers to break the encryption in the future.

Experts suggest that organizations need to start planning for this now because the transition to quantum-safe encryption will take time and rely on third-party upgrades. While quantum computing won’t be an immediate threat, industries with sensitive data, like governments and banks, are already taking action.

Some experts recommend understanding where encrypted data is stored and how it’s protected, while others advise focusing on current security vulnerabilities. A flexible approach to cryptography, called "crypto-agility," will allow organizations to adapt to new security algorithms as quantum computing advances.

The report stresses the need to act now to avoid future risks, as quantum computing could pose a trillion-dollar threat if organizations aren’t prepared.
